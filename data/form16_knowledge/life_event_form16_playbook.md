# Form 16 Life Event Playbook

## Bonus and Variable Pay

When a salaried user receives a bonus, the first document check is whether the
bonus has already been included in salary income and TDS. If the payout is
inside the payroll year and already reflected in Form 16 or the latest payslip,
the user should treat the money as post-tax cash. If it is not yet reflected,
they should hold back a tax reserve before investing it.

For a bonus-led decision, the safe order is:

1. ring-fence tax
2. top up the emergency fund
3. reduce high-interest debt
4. then send the long-term portion to goals or SIPs

## Job Switch and Dual Employer Cases

During a job switch, a single Form 16 may be incomplete. The user should
reconcile:

- previous employer Form 16
- current employer Form 16
- Form 12B or salary disclosure shared with the new employer
- total TDS deducted across both employers
- EPF transfer or continuity details

If previous-employer income is not disclosed to the new employer, TDS may look
lower than the final tax liability. The user should not deploy joining bonus or
full and final settlement cash until the tax picture is reconciled.

## Marriage Checklist

Marriage usually changes health cover, emergency-fund needs, nominee details,
and shared-goal ownership. Before committing extra SIPs or new EMIs, the couple
should document how expenses are split, who uses remaining 80C or NPS headroom,
and whether a family floater is needed.

## New Baby Checklist

A new baby usually increases monthly burn, pushes health-cover needs up, and
raises the minimum emergency-fund target. The user should review family floater
coverage, term cover, nominee details, and monthly childcare costs before
starting a child-goal SIP.

## Inheritance Notes

Inheritance is generally not read from Form 16 because it is not salary income.
The relevant discipline is different: keep paperwork and liquidity first, do not
rush into one-shot deployment, and update nominee, ownership, and estate records
alongside the financial plan.
